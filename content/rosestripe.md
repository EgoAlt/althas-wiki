**Summary**: Player character. A [[faeries|faerie]] messenger of the [[convent-of-saint-trefan|Convent of Saint Trefan]] in [[drinmery|Drinmery]], drawn into noble politics after intercepting a threatening letter aimed at her home.

> [!note] Player character
> Rosestripe is a PC, not an NPC.

A [[faeries|faerie]] of the Convent of Saint Trefan, unusual for her kind in being genuinely curious about the world: she taught herself to read so she could study scripture without waiting on the monks, and picks up outside books for their own sake, a trait both the monks and her fellow faeries write off as eccentric but harmless. She serves as the convent's messenger, carrying letters between local nobles, work she'd never had reason to think twice about.

That changed when a delivery went wrong. Caught in high wind on her way to deliver a response from a lord to a count, she was thrown against a tree, and the pouch carrying the letter tore open. The envelope was already ruined, and faeries who report a lost letter are believed more readily than ones who deliver a damaged one, so the plan was simple: destroy it and say it never arrived. Then she saw the name through the torn, translucent paper: St. Trefan. Her home.

She opened it anyway. It was a threat: the convent and its treasures would be seized by force unless a new trade agreement was signed. Rosestripe, who'd never cared about the affairs of humans "on the other side of the map," realized there was a wider world bearing directly on her own, and made a decision on the spot: she would start reading the letters she carried, learn what threatened her home, and stop it, whatever that cost her.

Who sent the letter isn't stated in the source material. [[guilmore-fleming|Count Guilmore Fleming]] and [[immanuel-greene|Lord Immanuel Greene]] are the two local nobles on record, both plausible, but which one (if either) is behind it is unknown until it comes up in play.
