**Summary**: [[the-holy-see|The Holy See]]'s investigative and judicial arm, now split into two warring factions after [[the-ophanim|the Ophanim]]'s landing shattered the Church's certainty: the old order and the radical Reborn Inquisition.

---

The Canton first rose as the hidden hand of [[the-holy-see|the Holy See]]: investigating corruption, exposing forbidden rites, and carrying sacred judgment into places ordinary law couldn't reach. Feared, but understood as a necessary instrument of protection. Their writs can unseat a lord, shame a priest, or drag a sinner screaming from beneath his own roof.

## The schism

Everything changed when [[the-ophanim|the Ophanim]] descended on [[hilltop|Hilltop]]. Many within the Inquisition could no longer believe the old ways were enough. A radical movement spread through the ranks, preaching that mercy had made them weak and that sin could not simply be confessed, it had to be drowned, erased, and remade.

The Canton is now one body with two hearts. The old order still sits in its chambers with seals, ledgers, and law, calling itself the true servant of the Holy See. The new order, the Reborn Inquisition, calls them cowards in clean robes. Near two in every three Inquisitors have already crossed over. No banner has been raised and no war declared, but chapels bar their doors against rival confessors, tribunals split down the middle, and in one town an Inquisitor may arrive to cleanse a heresy only to find another already there to name him heretic first.

## The Rite of Passage

The Reborn rite doesn't ask an initiate to serve. It kills him first. Before the sacred waters, the initiate speaks an oath broken into many vows, one for each trial ahead. Then he's drowned: name taken, memories stripped, and what rises is an Inquisitor without a past, filled with law, silence, and judgment.

Each vow fulfilled afterward returns a fragment of the drowned life: a face, a betrayal, a kindness too soft to survive the water, the returning old self and the newly-formed mind coexisting and growing alongside each other. Some initiates break when the memories start returning. Those who complete every vow keep both lives at once, the old self returns whole, but the Inquisitor remains alongside it, two full minds sharing one body. The new order calls them apostles. Others call them monsters. [[uriel-kenan|Uriel Kenan]] is mid-pilgrimage, not yet one or the other. Only the Kingslayer has ever completed it fully.

## The Seven Edicts of the Reborn Inquisition

1. **Keep the oath above the self.** Memory, desire, grief, and love may return, but none may rule.
2. **Respect law, but serve judgment.** Courtesy isn't weakness; no crown or priestly office outranks judgment.
3. **Take no cruelty as proof of strength.** Violence is a tool, not a feast.
4. **Let no sin sleep.** A hidden sin left buried becomes a forest.
5. **Protect the innocent, but not their lies.** Mercy is not blindness.
6. **Deal openly, and let thy word bind thee.** A blade that lies to itself cannot cut true.
7. **Through passion, master the chain.** Grief, rage, and fear are mastered, not buried.
