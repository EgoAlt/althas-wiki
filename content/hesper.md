---
title: Hesper
---

**Summary**: Archmage of the Triumvirate governing [[polaris|Polaris]], eccentric collector of odd things, and [[rastaban|Rastaban]]'s teacher. Vanished during a duel with her own son, Izar; her fate is unknown.

An older mage who held a seat on Polaris's ruling Triumvirate of Archmages, though her own reputation in the city leaned more eccentric than powerful: she was known for a quiet, unambitious lifestyle and a habit of collecting odd things purely to study them, rather than for anything resembling political weight. She believed magic was everywhere, for anyone with the eyes (or, as it turned out, the hands) to see it.

She found [[rastaban|Rastaban]] struggling and conspicuous in Polaris and took him in over a cup of tea, saw through his disguise immediately, and made him her student anyway once she'd heard his real story, the strangest thing she'd ever come across to study. She taught him everything he could absorb (history, geography, politics) even though his low aptitude as a giant kept the sacred texts themselves out of reach, and eventually took him to the exact center stone of Polaris to make her point: talent, like magic, doesn't need eyes.

She had a son, [[izar|Izar]], a mage who periodically challenged her for her Triumvirate seat. Polaris society read these duels as something close to a mercy Izar extended an "old stubborn woman," since she always won and always refused to kill him. When Ras carved sacred words into his own skin to work around his inability to read them, Hesper was horrified and begged him to stop before they understood the consequences, but that conversation never got the chance to finish.

## Disappearance

Izar came again to challenge her, this time unnaturally powerful and fast, changed by something unexplained (possibly his wand). Ras was told to stay back; when Izar badly wounded her, Ras intervened anyway. Hesper vanished in the aftermath, her house left in shambles, Ras missing an arm, and Izar badly hurt. What actually happened to her is not established in the source material: Izar publicly blamed Ras for her death, but nothing confirms she's actually dead.
