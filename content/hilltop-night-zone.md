## What happened

The Ophanim didn't just devastate central Hilltop when it landed in 330 VR, being sun-related, its death a year later left a permanent, unnatural wound: the zone of its influence around its corpse has never seen daylight since. This is what actually caused Hilltop's mass exodus, the land in and around that zone became unlivable, not through ruin or monsters alone, but through the sun itself being gone.

## Who stayed, and what they became

Most of Hilltop fled. Some didn't, and the society they built inside the permanent dark is its own thing entirely, closer to Barovia from Curse of Strahd than to anything else in Althas: isolated, insular, shaped by a corruption its people live inside rather than merely fight.

Two touchstones for the tone being aimed at here, both explicitly named by Lucas while brainstorming:

- A city that's always nighttime, riffing on Gotham, from the *Invincible* comic/show.
- The *Dawnguard* DLC from *Skyrim*, where a vampire faction has a whole prophecy built around blotting out the sun and remaking the world in that image.

The oldest, richest, most devout noble houses of Hilltop, the same elven houses noted on [[hilltop|Hilltop's]] own page as fusing nobility with holy office, are the ones who stayed. Cut off from the sun and from the rest of Althas, they didn't merely endure, they corrupted, morally and physically, into something between leeches, parasites, and vampires. See below and [[the-fallen-houses|the fallen houses]]. Old Blood experimentation is a live candidate for what actually caused that corruption, not yet confirmed.

## Rumors of the corpse below

Some say the Ophanim's corpse at the center of this zone is a massive snake-like body extending down into the earth, and that the body itself hides a dungeon full of monsters, so-called "Ophanim spawn." No one has confirmed what, if anything, actually lies at its bottom.
