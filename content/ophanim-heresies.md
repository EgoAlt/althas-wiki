---
title: "Ophanim Heresies"
---

**Summary**: Reality-breaking powers tied to an Ophanim, heretical gifts "from [[the-ones-below|the ones below]]" rather than sanctioned [[miracles|Miracles]] from [[the-one-above|the One Above]]. Deliberately subvert the logic of every other magic system in Althas rather than extending it. Currently only tied to [[codex-magic|Codex]]; each domain is intended to eventually have its own Ophanim and its own version of this.

---

## The core idea

Where [[miracles|Miracles]] are sanctioned, hereditary, and bounded (a gift with a fixed, describable effect), Ophanim-tied powers are heresies: unsanctioned, tied to a specific Ophanim rather than a bloodline, and structurally superior. This mirrors Re:Zero's own hierarchy directly: Authorities can do things Divine Protections categorically cannot (stop time, erase a person's name from existence, rewrite cause-and-effect), and no amount of Divine Protection can overcome an Authority. The explicit example Lucas gave for the flavor being aimed at: a power that erases a target's name from reality, and the target simply stops existing. That's the register: not "more powerful magic," but magic that doesn't play by the same rules as the other three systems at all.

## What's borrowed from Witch Factors specifically (confirmed 2026-07-12: the compensation mechanic is adopted)

Re:Zero's Authorities come with mechanical texture beyond raw power, and Althas has confirmed it wants the compensation piece specifically. In Re:Zero's own version: a Witch Factor "chooses" people naturally suited to it (compatible bearers), who can use their Authority freely; anyone else who ends up wielding one anyway (an incompatible bearer, through theft, force, or accident) has to pay a cost every time to draw on it, something of real value or influence over the world, decided case by case, not a fixed price. The natural bearer pays nothing; everyone else pays something.

Applied to Ophanim Heresies: whoever an Ophanim's power was "meant for" (however that's determined in Althas, not yet decided) uses it without cost. Anyone else who ends up bearing one instead, likely the more common and more dramatic case for player-facing purposes, has to give something up each time they use it. This is the actual mechanical hook, not just flavor: it means a PC or NPC wielding a heresy they weren't meant to have is making a real bargain every time, not just accessing a cool power for free.

Two more pieces of texture, also worth keeping in mind even though not explicitly confirmed as adopted:

- Each Witch Factor is tied to a "sin" or defining psychological theme, and actively tempts its bearer toward that theme rather than being a clean, neutral tool.
- The power itself can outlive whatever originally granted it (a Witch Factor's "door," once opened, stays open), which would give these powers the same "cost of power" shape already established for [[the-ophanim|the Ophanim's]] own mantle-succession.
