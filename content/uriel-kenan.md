title: Uriel Kenan
---

**Summary**: Player character. A Reborn Inquisitor of an oldblood [[hilltop|Hilltop]] house destroyed by [[the-ophanim|the Ophanim]], mid-pilgrimage to recover the man he was before the waters took his name.

> [!note] Player character
> Uriel Kenan is a PC, not an NPC.

Before the baptism, Uriel belonged to an oldblood elven line of [[hilltop|Hilltop]], a house where noble blood and holy office had fused so completely that no one remembered where one ended and the other began: relic-keepers, patrons, confessors, landholders, all loyal children of a faith that treated the house as part of the world's proper order.

That house is gone. Uriel wasn't at Hilltop when [[the-ophanim|the Ophanim]] descended and wiped it out; he never saw the sky open, never looked into the angel's eyes. Others were changed by witness. He was left with something worse: certainty of loss without the mercy of understanding. He believes the Ophanim was not a monster but punishment, that his house was judged and found wanting, the way a flood doesn't hate the houses it drowns. The Orthodox Canton teaches that mortal eyes should be covered before angels; Uriel calls that heresy, the purest insult a believer can offer, because he was never given the chance to look.

This is why he joined [[canton-of-inquisition|the Reborn Inquisition]]. His baptism was an execution: an oath broken into vows, then drowning. His old name, lineage, and memories were taken; what rose was a clean blade with no past to weaken it. They gave him a silver sword, Penance, and burial-shaped robes marked in blue, scripture on funeral cloth. He is not undead, he breathes and bleeds and hungers like anyone, but the metaphor is sacred: the old self was drowned, and what walks now carries itself like a corpse given duty.

He is still mid-pilgrimage. Each stage of the oath fulfilled returns a fragment of the drowned life, never mercy, just a memory, a wound, a face without a name. He's recovered enough to know his family mattered, that they're gone, and that he hates the old Inquisition's doctrine of blindness with a hatred that feels ancient rather than new. The Reborn don't want him healed by this. They want him dangerous: grief becomes doctrine, rage becomes discipline, guilt becomes a sharper prayer.

For thirty years he served as a soldier with an Inquisitor's authority in the [[jesthaen|Jesthaen]] rebellion, and he does not soften what that meant. Prisoners who didn't rise after questioning. Homes searched until they stopped being homes. He believed it necessary and still does, but memory returns anyway, some nights as smoke, some as screams. He killed an Inquisitor of the old order once, not out of hatred for the man himself, but because the schism demanded it and among the Reborn the act was obedience, not monstrosity.

What makes him dangerous is that he's calm. No village-prophet foaming, no need to shout. He speaks like a preacher at a funeral, and can sit in silence so complete that others start confessing just to fill it. But there are wounds under the stillness: family, lost houses, survivors denied the truth of what destroyed them. Press on those, and the saint cracks and the zealot shows through. He is not without compassion, cases that mirror his own move him more than he can control, but his mercy isn't gentle. He doesn't comfort wounds. He gives them purpose.

He doesn't know his birth name. Doesn't know if his house deserved what came to Hilltop. Doesn't know what the final recovered memory will make of him: redeemed, broken, or something worse.
