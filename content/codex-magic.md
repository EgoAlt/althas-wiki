**Summary**: How [[codex-magic|Codex]] magic works in Althas: casters draw a precise seal that tears a hole in the fabric of reality, letting power filter through from a parallel dimension. A fireball isn't conjured fire, it's a tear that happens to express itself as fire. Tied to Codex's own dead, splintered god, whose power now forms the barrier holding back [[the-ones-below|the ones below]].

---

## The core idea

Codex casters don't generate an effect, they open one. Drawing the correct seal tears a small hole in reality, and whatever bleeds through that hole expresses itself according to the seal's shape: a fireball is not a caster making fire, it's them tearing a hole shaped so that what comes out reads as fire. This is the deliberate opposite of [[splendor-magic|Splendor magic]]: Splendor asks a person (a god) for an effect; Codex reaches past people entirely into a place.

## The mechanic (from Witch Hat Atelier and AonDor)

Two source systems supply the actual grammar of how a seal works, and they combine cleanly:

- **Shape determines effect** (Witch Hat Atelier): a seal has a central sigil (the element/nature of the effect) and surrounding signs (how it manifests: as a column, a burst, a wall, etc.), enclosed in a ring that must be complete for the seal to activate. Seals can be linked, nested inside one another, or drawn in reverse to produce the opposite effect. An incorrectly drawn seal just fizzles rather than doing something unpredictable, magic here is precise, not chaotic.
- **A keyed, distance-limited conduit** (AonDor): the power isn't infinite or freely available everywhere, it's drawn through something like AonDor's Aons: shapes tuned to a specific source and weakening the further a caster strays from wherever that source is anchored. This gives Codex magic a natural geographic or ritual constraint worth using: a caster far from [[codex-magic|Codex's]] anchor point (not yet named) should be measurably weaker at it.

## Where the power actually comes from (from Divinity: Original Sin)

The "parallel dimension" isn't just flavor text, it mirrors Divinity: Original Sin's Source and the Veil: a substance harvested from living/soul energy, dangerous to misuse, sealed behind a barrier that keeps something worse out. In Althas's case:

- Codex's own patron god died in the Divine Age (or its aftermath), splintered like a Cosmere Shard rather than simply ending. This mirrors the design pattern already established for [[the-ophanim|the Ophanim's]] mantle-succession: a dead source of power that persists and can still be drawn on or taken up.
- That splintered power currently forms the barrier holding [[the-ones-below|the ones below]] sealed away. Codex casters are, whether they know it or not, drawing on the same substance that is also the prison wall.
- Source corruption in Divinity: Original Sin turns overusers into monstrous "sorcerers." Lucas has separately floated attributing shapeshifter monsters in Althas to this same corruption (see Old Blood for the parallel vampire/werewolf origin via a different mechanism).
