A nation of scholars and mages who broke off from Hilltop to pursue arcane ambition. Home to the continent's magic academies (Loreborne communities) and a magocratic industrial rise built on Giant labor. Rival of [[voldaen|Voldaen]], supported the [[jesthaen|Jesthaen]] rebels.

See [[althas]] for the full history and the Giants' role in Polaris society.

## Founding and the Giants' betrayal

Northern giant communities were promised a shared city, built by their own labor, where giants and Smallfolk would live together and giants would receive magic in return. Many giants died completing that labor over generations. When Polaris was finished, the giants who built it were turned away at the gate: the old deals ignored, their role reduced to a permanent, non-magical underclass, with not being arrested for trespassing offered as the only mercy. The giants who refused to accept that role retreated to the mountains and formed [[draconis|Draconis]] instead.

## Governance: the Triumvirate of Archmages

Polaris is ruled by a Triumvirate of Archmages. [[hesper|Hesper]], one of the three, held her seat with a reputation for a quiet, unambitious lifestyle rather than political ambition, and was repeatedly (unsuccessfully) challenged for it by her own son, [[izar|Izar]]. Little else about the Triumvirate's other members or its actual power structure is established yet.

The exact center of the city, where its first stone was laid, is treated as a site of some significance: the city was reputedly built without instruments, no plans, no stone ever misplaced or adjusted.
