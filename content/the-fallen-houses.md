title: "The Fallen Houses"
---

**Summary**: The ancient elven noble houses of [[hilltop|Hilltop]], once holy office-holders who fought alongside the One Above in the Divine Age's holy war, who stayed behind in [[hilltop-night-zone|the Hilltop night zone]] after the Ophanim's death and corrupted into something between leeches, parasites, and vampires. "The Fallen Houses" is a working name pending an in-fiction one.

---

## What they were

Ancient elven bloodlines that fused nobility with holy office in old Hilltop: relic-keepers and confessors as much as lords, the richest, most noble, and most holy families the city had. Some of these houses fought at the One Above's side during the Divine Age's holy war. [[uriel-kenan|Uriel Kenan]]'s own house was one of the noble-holy families of Hilltop, though it was destroyed in the ruin rather than joining the ones described here.

## What they became

Cut off from the sun inside [[hilltop-night-zone|the night zone]], these houses didn't flee and didn't simply endure, they corrupted, both morally and physically, into something between leeches, parasites, and vampires. They now look down on the mortal races they once served, believing the One Above doesn't want mortals in his kingdom at all.

The tone being aimed at: the fall of a family, not a monster showing up from outside. The richest, most devout, most trusted names in Hilltop turning into exactly what they used to guard against, made worse by centuries (or however long it's been) stewing in a permanent dark of their own making.

## Open questions

Not yet decided, flagged here rather than invented:

- How many houses fell this way, and whether any specific named house or NPC has been chosen yet, none has, so far.
- The literal mechanical nature of the corruption ("leeches, parasites, vampires" is the vibe given, not yet a settled monster type or Daggerheart adversary stat block, though Old Blood's dosage/willpower mechanic is a strong candidate).
- How long ago this corruption set in relative to 331 VR (the Ophanim's death) and the campaign's present day.
