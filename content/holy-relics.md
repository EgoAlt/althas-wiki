---
title: "Holy Relics"
---

**Summary**: Relics from the Divine Age, usable only by those with holy blood ([[miracles|Miracles]]). Modeled directly on Fire Emblem: Three Houses' Hero's Relics and Crests, including the consequence for misuse: transformation into a monster. Also drawing on the Cosmere's Shardblades and Shardplate: hereditary, extremely powerful, and fought over across generations.

---

## The core idea

Relics from the Divine Age's war against [[the-ones-below|the ones below]] can only be safely wielded by someone with holy blood, a [[miracles|Miracle]]. This is the direct parallel to Fódlan's own Hero's Relics and Crests: a Relic paired with its matching Crest works as intended; without it, using the Relic is dangerous.

## Consequence for unworthy use

Modeled directly on Fódlan's own rule: misusing a Relic without the matching Miracle risks transforming the wielder into a monster. Fódlan's version (Fódlan's own Hero's Relics) has misuse at low health transform the wielder into a Demonic Beast, reverting only when that beast form is slain, and killing the wielder along with it if the beast dies. Althas's version of this consequence hasn't been worked out in the same mechanical detail yet, but the shape (transformation, danger to self and others, no easy undo) is the intended fit.

## Hereditary, and fought over: the Shardblade parallel

Beyond the Fire Emblem parallel, these Relics are meant to function like the Cosmere's Shardblades and Shardplate: extraordinarily powerful, passed down within families rather than found or crafted freely, and a real driver of war and politics in their own right, houses and nations fighting over who holds which Relic, not just what it does in a single fight.

## A specific example: the Berserker Armor

Proposed as [[voldaen|House Voldis's]] own hereditary Relic: their Shardplate-equivalent, a suit of armor as strong as it is old, modeled on Guts's Berserker Armor from *Berserk* (also a touchstone for [[edrion-voldis|Edrion Voldis]] himself, see that page). Whether Edrion wore it during the Ophanim fight, and what its own cost (Berserker Armor, in its source, isn't a clean power-up) looks like in Althas are all open.

## Open questions

Not yet decided, flagged here rather than invented:

- How many named Relics exist, and which families or nations hold which.
- The exact mechanical shape of the transformation-on-misuse consequence in Daggerheart terms.
- Whether every Relic is tied to a specific named Miracle, the way Fódlan's Relics pair one-to-one with specific Crests.
