**Summary**: An ancestry bound almost entirely to church and convent life, their very origins a secret kept by the Holy See. Trusted across Althas as message carriers precisely because of how little they care about anyone's business but their flock's.

---

No scholar has a solid account of where faeries come from, and how they are created is a closely guarded secret of [[the-holy-see|the Holy See]].

Faeries are peaceful and remarkably uninterested in the world outside their own flock. Even sociable ones manage nothing warmer than a cold, distant friendship with a local church member; most keep to other faeries entirely. This incuriosity is likely exactly why they became Althas's message carriers: no one else could be trusted not to read a private letter out of curiosity, but a faerie handed an open letter would rather not bother. The only writing they take any real interest in is holy scripture and the (typically dull) writings of other faeries about flock life and church trivia.

Faeries not bound to a cleric are extremely rare. When it happens, it's usually trauma or survival driving it: a master who exaggerates punishment for late deliveries, or worse.

Exceptions to the incuriosity do turn up, a faerie who teaches itself to read beyond scripture, or takes real interest in the world outside the flock, but they're rare enough that the others treat such behavior as eccentric, if harmless.
