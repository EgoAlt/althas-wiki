**Summary**: [[the-holy-see|The Holy See]]'s investigative and judicial arm, now split into two warring factions after [[the-ophanim|the Ophanim]]'s landing shattered the Church's certainty: the old order and the radical Reborn Inquisition.

---

The Canton first rose as the hidden hand of [[the-holy-see|the Holy See]]: investigating corruption, exposing forbidden rites, and carrying sacred judgment into places ordinary law couldn't reach. Feared, but understood as a necessary instrument of protection. Their writs can unseat a lord, shame a priest, or drag a sinner screaming from beneath his own roof.

## The schism

Everything changed when [[the-ophanim|the Ophanim]] descended on [[hilltop|Hilltop]]. Many within the Inquisition could no longer believe the old ways were enough. A radical movement spread through the ranks, preaching that mercy had made them weak and that sin could not simply be confessed, it had to be drowned, erased, and remade.

The Canton is now one body with two hearts. The old order still sits in its chambers with seals, ledgers, and law, calling itself the true servant of the Holy See. The new order, the Reborn Inquisition, calls them cowards in clean robes. Near two in every three Inquisitors have already crossed over. No banner has been raised and no war declared, but chapels bar their doors against rival confessors, tribunals split down the middle, and in one town an Inquisitor may arrive to cleanse a heresy only to find another already there to name him heretic first.
