**Summary**: Hereditary gifts granted by [[the-one-above|the One Above]] to allies during the Divine Age's war against [[the-ones-below|the ones below]]. A Miracle grants the recipient holy blood, which can (not guaranteed) pass to descendants. Explains why [[voldaen|House Voldis]] is as powerful as it is: not simple longevity or training, but generations of hoarded, inherited Miracles.

---

## The core idea

During the Divine Age, [[the-one-above|the One Above]] granted Miracles to people who fought alongside him against [[the-ones-below|the ones below]]. A Miracle changes the recipient's blood: it becomes "holy," and that inheritance can (but does not always) pass down to their descendants. Most Miracles are small and strange rather than powerful, the equivalent of Re:Zero's Divine Protection of never mistaking salt for sugar, and only a small fraction are genuinely combat- or plot-relevant. Holding more than one Miracle naturally is exceptionally rare.

## Named Miracles established so far

- **Usurpation**: steals another person's Miracle for the user. Directly modeled on Re:Zero's Divine Protection of Usurpation. This is [[voldaen|House Voldis's]] own signature Miracle.
- **Sword Saint**: grants overwhelming, almost supernatural martial skill, modeled on Reinhard van Astrea's Divine Protection of the Sword Saint.
- **Phoenix**: grants a second life after death. Modeled on Reinhard's Divine Protection of the Phoenix.
- **Recurrence**: not yet mechanically defined beyond its narrative purpose: paired with Phoenix, it keeps resetting/refreshing the Phoenix effect rather than letting it exhaust after one use, producing something close to true immortality rather than a single extra life. See [[edrion-voldis|Edrion Voldis]] for the specific character this is tied to.
