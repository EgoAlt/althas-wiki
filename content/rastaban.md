**Summary**: Player character. A [[draconis|Draconis]] giant, wizard of the School of Knowledge, serving penal labor for the church after being framed for killing his archmage teacher, Hesper.

> [!note] Player character
> Rastaban ("Ras") is a PC, not an NPC.

23 years old, born in [[draconis|Draconis]], son of [[thuban|Thuban]] and younger brother of [[eltanin|Eltanin]]. Big and strong, but visibly damaged: only his left arm remains, his left eye is scarred, and both it and his rare third eye (a Giant "Strange Pattern") stay hidden under a detailed blindfold-headband. Long, unkempt hair.

Where Eltanin took after their father, stoic and content with Draconis's survival-first philosophy, Ras never accepted that the giants' birthright to Polaris and its magic had been forfeited. At 17, when Thuban retired the mantle to Eltanin, Ras left for Polaris to claim what he believed was his people's due.

## Arrival in Polaris

Polaris wasn't built for a giant, disguised or not: he broke things, couldn't speak the local tongue well, and no one would teach him. He was taken in by [[hesper|Hesper]], an archmage of the Triumvirate who found him out immediately and made him her student anyway, the "weirdest thing" she'd ever found to study.

Giants have a low aptitude for magic, and Ras was no exception: he memorized everything else Hesper taught him (geography, history, politics) but couldn't learn to read the sacred texts. Hesper took him to the exact center of Polaris, to the first stone the giants ever laid, and told him the same lesson the city itself proved: talent, like magic, is everywhere, not just where eyes can see it.

## The breakthrough, and the cost

After months of study, Ras found his own way in: tracing the sacred texts by touch instead of sight, using a thicker ink. It worked, he could finally read magic, and Hesper gifted him a proper mage's headband to replace his old disguise. But he still couldn't cast.

Desperate, he spent a night carving key words from the sacred texts into his own skin. Hesper was horrified and begged him to stop until they understood the consequences. Ras never got the chance to find out what those consequences were on his own terms.

## The night Hesper disappeared

[[izar|Izar]], Hesper's son, came to challenge her for her Triumvirate seat again, this time unnaturally powerful, faster, changed by something no one has explained (possibly his wand). Ras was told to stay back while Hesper fought, but when Izar badly wounded her, Ras intervened. His right arm filled with a burning pain; when he could see again, Hesper had vanished, the house was wrecked, Izar was badly hurt, and Ras's arm was gone.

Izar struck first, scarring Ras's eye, and immediately began telling anyone who'd listen that a "horrible giant" had invaded and murdered an archmage, and that he himself had struck the giant down. Ras was arrested without resistance. Interrogators, deeming him too "broken" to question properly but too dangerous to release, tried repeatedly to remove his skin-carved markings; when washing failed, they tried skinning him alive, more than once, stopping only when nothing but live flesh remained. Nobody in the giant communities they questioned would admit to knowing a "Rastaban."

## Current status

Three years into penal servitude for the church, doing manual labor (heavy lifting, clearing magically hazardous areas) to avoid using magic at all. That changed once: during a rescue, a boulder threatened civilians and Ras, panicking, summoned a blue arcane arm from his missing shoulder and shattered it. Since then his assignments have leaned more dangerous, mostly clearing magical hazards and scaring off (not killing, by his own restraint) beasts.

He's still watched closely: his left hand is bound to his own right shoulder to stop him casting, and his headband stays on to hide the third eye and the scar. Every few days his captors attempt to skin the markings again. It never gets easier.

## Character

Ancestry: Giant. Community: Loreborne. Class: Wizard, subclass School of Knowledge. Strange Pattern: three eyes. Class item: a book of his own markings, plus whatever texts of Hesper's (or found along the way) he's still trying to translate.

Traits: Knowledge 2, Strength 1, Finesse 1, Instinct 0, Agility 0, Presence -1.
