**Summary**: The arm of [[the-holy-see|the Holy See]] that presides over doctrine, scripture, and the proper form of worship. Where [[parish-of-inquisition|the Inquisition]] judges sin, Orthodoxy defines it, and guards the sacred words that Splendor magic itself depends on.

---

The Parish of Orthodoxy is the doctrinal heart of [[the-holy-see|the Holy See]]. It settles what scripture means, what worship should look like, and where the line between faith and heresy falls. Most of the faithful meet it only through its rulings, handed down and enforced by other Parishes, but every one of those rulings begins here.

## Keepers of the Word

Orthodoxy's quiet power comes from what it holds. [[splendor-magic|Splendor Magic]] is not a caster's own strength but a request answered by [[the-one-above|the One Above]], and the words that make a request work are fixed, ancient, and few. They are discovered, not invented, and most of them sit in the Parish's keeping, in scripture the Parish alone is trusted to study and decipher. Whoever controls that research controls what Splendor magic can even do. It is a slow, jealous kind of authority, measured in decades of decoding rather than open displays of force, and it makes Orthodoxy one of the most powerful arms of the Church without ever raising a hand.

## The covered eye

Orthodox doctrine teaches that mortal eyes should be covered before the divine, that to look on an angel unveiled is presumption. The teaching shapes the Parish's rites, its art, and the veils and masks its highest clergy wear in the presence of the sacred. The fall of [[the-ophanim|the Ophanim]] on [[hilltop|Hilltop]] has made the doctrine a fault line rather than a comfort: some now ask whether the angel was a herald to be beheld or a judgment no one should have survived seeing.

## The Pontiff

The faith's supreme authority is the Pontiff, chosen by election of the Church's clerics and, by long habit, from among the deepest scholars of Orthodoxy. Election has never been blind to blood: a few houses carry [[miracles|Miracles]] so suited to the office that their names recur across the roll of Pontiffs. The chair is held now by [[amalthus-cruoris|Amalthus Cruoris]], a scriptural scholar of rare depth and bearer of his house's [[miracles|Miracle of Viaticum]].
